Our Maori Whanau

The Maori from the Northland region are as diverse as the glorious grub that comes from the region. Here you will find the Ngāi Takoto, Ngā Puhi, Ngāti Whātua, Ngāti Kahu, Ngāti Kurī, Ngāti Wai, Te Aupōuri, Te Rarawa Te Roroa. The region was one of the first placesto be settled by the Maori that arrived from far across the Pacific. These people arrived certainly arrived at a fine spot. The climate was temperate and as close to the conditions they left when they bid farewell to their ancestors in Hawaiki. They brought crops and livestock with them and were successful farmers. However, they also found the country a cornucopia for native goods. Fishing and hunting were popular and kai from the sea and rivers is still an important taonga for these people today.
