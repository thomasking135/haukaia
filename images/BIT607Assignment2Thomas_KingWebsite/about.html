Ko Wai Maatou | About The Haukai Restaurant

The Haukai Restaurant began in 2017 to bring authentic Maori kai to our community here in the Bay of Islands. We were passionate about sharing our knowledge of Maori food preparation which is an important part of our identity. We saw a haikai or craving for food which reflects the love more and more New Zealanders share for traditions which can be found nowhere else on Earth. We are about traditions and preserving Maori ways, but we also see ourselves at pushing the boundaries of the future of New Zealand gastronomy. We want to play a part in infusing our food with the flavours of one land and many people. From serving food at farmers markets on weekends, we moved to our present bricks and mortar site where we are challenging the status quo of what Maori and New Zealand kai are all about. Located in the heart of Kerikeri, the town that was so nice it had to be named twice in the beautiful Bay of Plenty, we will become one of the many wonders you discover in the heart-stopping Northland region.


Tēnā koe - Meet Mein Hosts Mia and Mateo

Mia and Mateo have loved cooking for as long as they can remember. Mateo grew up working on the land while Mia was never far away from her nana and the wondrous smells and stories that came from her kitchen. It was the power of nana’s cooking and the insatiable appetite of Mateo for Mia’s good cooking at the local marae that brought these love birds together (with a little help from nana along the way). United by a common love for scrumptious Maori food, both saw an opportunity to reconnect people across Aotearoa through the incredible power of food. They believe that Maori kai has been overlooked by the public for far too long and New Zealanders and visitors alike are missing out on something very special. Both Mia and Mateo pride themselves in providing top quality, fresh, wholesome, Maori dishes and a unique cultural awakening that will bring everyone the joy they experienced growing up with their loved ones. Let their table bring you and your whanau together the way it brought Mia and Mateo together.
