Welcome to historic Kerikeri

Welcome to Kerikeri, where bicultural Aotearoa began. This is a place that became the cradle of the nation and established early relationships between Pakeha Europeans and the Maori of the Northland province. It is no surprise why both were attracted to this beautiful part of Aotearoa. With its agreeably pleasant tropical climate, Kerikeri was an inviting place for any settler to make a home. Kerikeri is where the first mission was established in Aotearoa bringing Maori and Pakeha together in a celebration of religion, learning, and food. The mission was impressed by the Maori and became one of the strongest defenders for respecting the rights of the indigenous people of Aotearoa. The town of Kerikeri is the home of several important historical buildings that captures the forging of bicultural relations between Maori and Pakeha through it’s religious and farming legacy. These include the exquisite Stone Store, St James Church, Mission house and Rewa’s Village. Maori often joke that the origins of the naming of the town highlight the Maori patience for Pakeha to finally pronounce it tika (correct). To various European ears, Kerikeri sounded like Caddiecaddie, Keddikeddi and even the missionaries thought they heard it as Kiddikiddi. Over the years, Maori agree that the Pakeha pronunciation has certainly gone up a notch.
